CSC110 Syllabus

Assignments

Projects

I will assign several projects during the semester, and I've created a separate website covering the details of these projects. Each project falls into one of the following categories:

  • A Guided Project provides step-by-step instructions for writing a program. These steps won't tell you exactly what to code, but they break the program into a series of smaller problems that you can solve one at a time.
  • An Expanded Project builds on previous Guided Projects to create a more complete program. It is significantly more complex than a Guided Project, and you will not be given step-by-step directions for completing each task. You also have the option to propose an alternative version of the assignment.
  • A Self-Directed Project requires you to design your own program and submit a proposal describing it. Self-Directed Projects are optional and can be used to replace any of the other projects.

Project Submission

All projects must be submitted on Blackboard, and most projects must pass a set of tests to be considered complete. To make it easier for me to check new submissions frequently, I require projects to be zipped in a particular format. I will provide a program that checks whether your project passes these tests and then zips your project in the correct format.

If a project submission is improperly zipped, is missing any tasks/deliverables, or does not pass the required test cases, then that submission will be marked as incomplete. You may resubmit the project as long as you follow the restrictions in the deadlines section below.

Deadlines

I don't like assigning deadlines for assignments unless there is a compelling reason that I need your work by a particular time or need to otherwise limit submissions. If I do set a deadline or other restriction, then I will explain why.

The following restrictions apply to project submissions:

  • You are allowed to submit Guided Project assignments as often as you like until 11:59 PM ET on Sunday, December 8th.
  • You can submit up to one proposal for a Self-Directed Project or an alternative Expanded Project every week.
  • You can submit up to one Self-Directed Project or Expanded Project (I'll refer to these as the open-ended projects) each week.
  • If a project requires me to approve a proposal, then you cannot submit that project until the week after you submit the proposal.
  • The cutoff for weekly proposal/project submissions is 11:59 PM ET on Sunday. If you submit after this time, then it will count as your submission for the following week.
  • The last weekly proposal deadline is 11:59 PM ET on Sunday, November 24th.
  • No projects or proposals will be accepted 11/25-12/1 (Thanksgiving break).
  • The last weekly project deadline is 11:59 PM ET on Sunday, December 8th.
  • I will not accept any late submissions and will strictly enforce all deadlines. The deadline for Guided Projects and final weekly deadline for open-ended projects is your last opportunity to submit work for this class.

My reasons for these restrictions:

  • The Guided Project assignments can be fully checked by my test program and a couple of scripts, so they take little time for me to review. I leave myself a week at the end of the semester to determine final grades, review projects one last time, and resolve academic integrity cases.
  • I chose to go with weekly project/proposal submissions instead of project-specific deadlines to keep the open-ended projects more flexible. The limit of one per week ensures I don't get flooded with too many late in the semester. I aim to review them within a week of the cut-off so you can have timely feedback.
  • I give specific times and enforce deadlines strictly to ensure I handle assignment submissions consistently and fairly. It doesn't matter how close you are to a missed deadline or what excuse you offer. You either submit the assignment on time or arrange for an extension (see below); otherwise, I will not accept the assignment.

Extensions and Other Exceptions

In rare cases (such as medical or family emergencies), I will allow an extension to a deadline or an exception to another restriction on submitting an assignment. You can email me to ask for a such an exception if you believe one is appropriate in your situation. Your email should include an explanation of why you need the extension, a new proposed deadline (or other proposed change to existing restrictions), and your current progress on the assignment. Be sure to send me your code and any other material you've created for the assignment so far.

I have some expectations if I am going to give an extension or make an exception to a class policy:

  • You've demonstrated that you read this policy by including all of the information I just asked for.
  • If you're having trouble with the assignment, then you're taking steps to get help. Usually this means seeing a tutor or going to office hours.
  • You are clearly making an effort to keep up with the class and not procrastinating. If there is an ongoing problem affecting your work or participation in the class and you want me to accommodate you, then you must let me know early and keep me informed as that situation develops.

Extensions and other exceptions are given on a case-by-case basis. I will try to be as fair as possible, but this comes down to my judgement and whether you've been proactively communicating with me.

Advice

If you struggle with deadlines or time management, then I'd like to offer some advice:

  • Start your work early. You have four to six assignments to complete depending on your desired grade, and you probably won't be ready to start them for the first few weeks of class. It's not a big workload if you spread it out, but it will be overwhelming if you let it pile up towards the end of the semester.
  • Deadlines in this class are not targets. If I give you a deadline, then this is the latest that I'm willing to accept the assignment under most circumstances. I expect you to finish and submit the assignment much earlier. If you plan to submit something the night it's due, then there's a good chance you'll miss that deadline. I can't stop you from taking this risk, but you have to accept the consequences if it doesn't work out.
  • There are people you can talk to if you need help. Talk to someone when you start having trouble; don't wait until you feel hopelessly stuck or overwhelmed. And if you did wait until you were overwhelmed, that doesn't mean it's too late to get help! That just means it's even more urgent that you ask for help now, and that it may take more effort to catch up.
    • I'm available by email and during office hours.
    • The Learning Commons employs peer tutors who can help you with this class.
    • Academic coaches can be reached by email and are available by appointment. They may not be able to help you with your code, but they're great to talk to if you have trouble with time management or adjusting to college.
  • Keep backups of your work in case you have a problem with your computer. Google Drive and Dropbox both have free plans, and I believe you can use OneDrive for free through your QU account. You can also download any work you previously submitted on Blackboard.

Other Assignments

There will be other assignments in addition to the projects. For example, I may ask you to read a section of my notes, view a video, or complete a practice activity. While I may not deduct points from your grade if you miss these assignments, ignoring reading and practice assignments may result in you not acquiring the necessary skills and knowledge to complete the major projects, which means they can still indirectly affect your final grade. I try not to assign work frivilously, and I expect you to complete assignments whether or not I attach a grade to them.
